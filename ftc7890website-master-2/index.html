Our Robot

Our robot traverses the field using our AndyMark TileRunner Chassis. Our mechanum wheels allow us to move laterally, as well as forward and backwards, and our Cascading X-Rails allow us to quickly extend our Box Intake with compression wheels to efficiently obtain the cubes.

flash_on

Our Strategy

For autonomous we prioritize the usage of a range and gyro sensor to navigate the field. We have prioritized the elimination of “hard code” and strive to program a robot that be consistent even when the field isn’t by implementing state machines. Precise driver controls reduce error during the driver controlled phase.

Our Impact

7890 aims to spread the passion of robotics and computer science in our community using our passion, resources, and experiences. 7890 also wants to establish an altruistic presence by supporting causes outside of robotics and inside our community as a team.



Balancing Individual Achievement and a Caring Community


na

STEAM on the Hill is a sub-program of Summer on the Hill that allows children from low-income families to get the private school STEM education that they cannot afford. At STEAM on the Hill there are two classes, one where they teach Java, and one which teaches FLL.

Last year, our team took on the challenge of helping P.S./M.S 95 start and run their very own FLL team. Since then we’ve continued to help this team in various ways: we’ve showed them the challenge video, provided them with Lego Mindstorms kits, and explained to them how EV3 works.

Since the start of 7890 three years ago, we’ve been hosting bake sales at our school to fund different robotics teams in our neighborhood. In our first year we raise more than $1000, and this year we raised over $2000. We used that money to buy FLL kits for the George Jackson Academy.
